Learners Sample Questions

Question 1: What does a green traffic light indicate?

Answer: A green traffic light indicates that you can proceed, but you should yield the right-of-way to any pedestrians and oncoming traffic that may still be in the intersection.

Question 2: When should you dim your high beams while driving at night?

Answer: Dim your high beams when you are within 150 meters of an oncoming vehicle or within 60 meters of a vehicle you are following.

Question 3: When should you use your hazard lights (four-way flashers)?

Answer: Use hazard lights only when your vehicle is stationary, and there is a hazardous condition or obstruction on the road.

Question 4: What is the maximum speed limit for cars and motorcycles on a national highway in Kerala?

Answer: The maximum speed limit for cars and motorcycles on a national highway in Kerala is 80 kilometers per hour (kph).

Question 5: What should you do when you approach a roundabout?

Answer: Slow down, yield to traffic within the roundabout, and proceed in a counterclockwise direction.

Question 6: What is the legal blood alcohol concentration (BAC) limit for drivers in Kerala?

Answer: The legal BAC limit for drivers in Kerala is typically 0.03 grams per deciliter (g/dL) of blood.

Question 6: When is it mandatory to wear a seatbelt in a vehicle?

Answer: It is mandatory to wear a seatbelt in a moving vehicle, both for the driver and passengers, including those in the rear seats.

Question 7: What should you do if your vehicle's brakes fail?

Answer: Shift to a lower gear, use the handbrake gently, and look for a safe place to bring the vehicle to a stop.

Question 8:When are you allowed to overtake another vehicle on the left side?

Answer: Overtaking on the left side is allowed when the vehicle being overtaken is making or about to make a right turn, and it's safe to pass.
